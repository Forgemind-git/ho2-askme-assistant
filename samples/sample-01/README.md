# HO2 Sample 1 — Job Seeker

## What you'll build
A simple, good-looking personal web page that does your job-hunting for you. It shows who
you are, your skills, your best projects, and an **"Ask Me Anything"** section that answers
the questions recruiters always ask. The page in this folder is already filled in with a
realistic example (Maya Chen, a frontend developer) — open it, see it working, then swap in
your own details.

## Use it with your Claude.ai subscription
No API key needed. Just your normal Claude.ai login.

1. Open **`index.html`** from this folder in your browser. You'll see the finished example
   page. This is exactly what yours will look like once you put your own words in.
2. Open **Claude.ai** in another tab (your subscription — no API key, no billing).
3. Paste **the example prompt below** into Claude.ai and fill in the blanks with your real
   experience. Claude will write your About text, your skills list, and three strong
   recruiter answers for you.
4. Open `index.html` in a plain text editor (Notepad, TextEdit, or VS Code). Find the
   example name and text and **replace it with what Claude gave you**. Save the file.
5. Refresh the page in your browser to see your version.
6. (Optional) To get a shareable link: in your GitHub repo go to **Settings → Pages**,
   pick the `main` branch, and GitHub gives you a free live URL.

## The example prompt
Copy this into Claude.ai and fill in the parts in CAPITALS:

```
I'm building a one-page personal website to help me get hired. Help me write the content for it.

About me:
- Name: YOUR NAME
- The role I want: e.g. mid-level Frontend Developer
- My experience: e.g. 3 years building React apps at a small startup
- Top skills/tools: e.g. React, TypeScript, Next.js, testing
- Two or three projects I'm proud of: DESCRIBE EACH IN ONE LINE

Please give me:
1. A two-sentence "About Me" that sounds confident but honest.
2. A short title and one-line description for each project (what it was, what I did, the result).
3. Answers to these three recruiter questions, two or three sentences each:
   - "What is your strongest skill?"
   - "Why are you looking for a new role?"
   - "What kind of team do you do your best work in?"

Keep it warm, specific, and free of clichés.
```

## Make it your own
- Swap the colour: change `--color` near the top of `index.html` to your favourite hex code.
- Replace the three example projects with real ones — keep them short and outcome-focused.
- Add or remove skill chips so the list matches the job you're actually applying for.
