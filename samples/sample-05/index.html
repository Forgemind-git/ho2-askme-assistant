What you'll build

An AI-powered Claude Artifact — a small chat page that answers as you, grounded only in facts you give it. You build it by chatting with Claude in plain English: no code, no API key. Then you publish it as a shareable link. Anyone with a Claude account can open that link and ask questions, and Claude answers live from your material.

The finished example lower down plays Sofia Reyes, a science teacher moving into UX research, reframing her real experience toward the role she wants. Follow the steps below with your own facts to make your own.

Build it in Claude chat — step by step

  1. Turn on AI-powered artifacts

    In claude.ai, open Settings → Feature preview and switch on “Create AI-powered artifacts”. This lets an artifact call Claude at runtime (that's what makes the answers live). You only do this once.

  2. Start a new chat and paste the build prompt

    Open a fresh chat and paste the prompt below. It tells Claude to build the chat UI and pins it to your facts, so it reframes your real experience without ever inventing titles you don't have.

    Build an AI-powered artifact: a chat "Ask-Me" assistant that answers
as me, in the first person, using ONLY the facts below. Keep answers
warm, specific and 2-4 sentences. When asked why I'm qualified,
REFRAME my real experience toward the target role using only these
facts - never fabricate job titles, employers, dates or experience I
don't have. If asked about gaps, be honest. If something isn't in the
facts, say you don't have that detail. Give it a clean chat UI with a
few suggested questions and a message box.

    Make it yours: replace every line under “Facts about me” with your own past roles and the role you're targeting. Keep the “reads as” reframes honest — real work stated in the new field's language, never invented titles.

  3. Watch Claude render it as a live Artifact, and test it

    Claude builds the chat and it appears in the Artifact panel on the right — already interactive. Ask it three real questions, e.g. the ones below, and check every answer reframes your genuine experience and never invents a title or employer.

    Why are you qualified for a UX research role?
How does teaching transfer to UX?
What's a gap you're still working on?

    If an answer overclaims or invents detail, tell Claude in the same chat — e.g. “never say I held a UX job title” or “be honest about gaps” — and it rebuilds the artifact.

  4. Publish and share it

    Use the artifact's Publish / Share control to get a public link, then drop that link on your CV, LinkedIn or job applications. Two things to know: viewers need their own Claude account (answers run on their account, so it isn't an anonymous public bot), and the live answers only work inside claude.ai — the same file opened as a plain web page can't reach Claude.
