How should I handle a performance improvement plan?
ASSISTANT
Key steps for a fair, effective PIP: 1. Document first — Gather specific examples of the performance gap (dates, metrics, observed behaviour vs. expected standard). 2. Private conversation — Frame the PIP as a support mechanism, not a warning. Share the evidence and listen. 3. Set SMART goals — Specific, measurable targets with a 30/60/90-day timeline. Ambiguity causes disputes. 4. Weekly check-ins — Schedule brief, structured 1:1s during the PIP period. Document each one. 5. Define success and consequences clearly — Both "what good looks like" and what happens if targets aren't met. 6. Loop in Legal/ER early if the employment relationship is complex. Reminder: A PIP should feel like a genuine second chance, not a paper trail.

What are best practices for onboarding new hires?
ASSISTANT
The research-backed framework: • Week 1 — Clarity on role, team, and tools. No major deliverables. Focus on belonging. • Month 1 — First small win. Shadowing + listening mode. Manager 1:1 at least twice a week. • Month 3 — Independent contribution. 30-day check-in survey sent by HR. • Month 6 — Full ramp. Probation review tied to agreed milestones. Common mistakes to avoid: ✗ Overloading Day 1 with admin and compliance videos ✗ No buddy/peer assigned ✗ Manager unavailable in the first two weeks Tool tip: Use Claude to draft personalised welcome messages and 30-60-90 day plans tailored to each role.
YOU
How do I write a JD that attracts diverse candidates?
ASSISTANT
Evidence-based adjustments: 1. Audit language — Use a tool like Textio or ask Claude to flag gendered/exclusionary language. "Rockstar", "ninja", "fast-paced" deter underrepresented groups. 2. Requirements vs. nice-to-haves — Research shows women apply only when they meet ~100% of listed criteria; men apply at ~60%. Move stretch skills to "Nice to have". 3. Lead with impact — Start with what the role achieves, not a laundry list of duties. 4. State flexibility upfront — Remote/hybrid options, part-time consideration, reasonable adjustments. 5. Remove degree requirements where not genuinely needed — Focus on skills and demonstrated experience instead.
