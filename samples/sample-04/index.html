Booking speakers Answers live

Event organisers ask me the same booking questions over and over. Instead of a back-and-forth, ask my page — it answers what I speak about and whether I fit your event, live, from my real material.

What you'll build

An AI-powered Claude Artifact — a small chat page that answers as you, grounded only in facts you give it. You build it by chatting with Claude in plain English: no code, no API key. Then you publish it as a shareable link. Anyone with a Claude account can open that link and ask questions, and Claude answers live from your material.

The finished example lower down plays Marcus Bell, a keynote speaker, answering event-organiser questions. Follow the steps below with your own facts to make your own.

Build it in Claude chat — step by step

  1. Turn on AI-powered artifacts

    In claude.ai, open Settings → Feature preview and switch on “Create AI-powered artifacts”. This lets an artifact call Claude at runtime (that's what makes the answers live). You only do this once.

  2. Start a new chat and paste the build prompt

    Open a fresh chat and paste the prompt below. It tells Claude to build the chat UI and pins it to your facts so it can't make things up.

    Build an AI-powered artifact: a chat "Ask-Me" assistant that answers
as me, in the first person, using ONLY the facts below. Keep answers
warm, specific and 2-4 sentences. Never invent talks, events, claims
or fees that aren't in the facts. For fee questions, say fees depend
on format and travel and invite them to ask for a quote - never state
a number. If something isn't in the facts, say you don't have that
detail rather than guessing. Give it a clean chat UI with a few
suggested questions and a message box.

Facts about me:
- Name: Marcus Bell - keynote speaker & workshop host on practical
  AI adoption for non-technical teams.
- 9 years in operations before going full-time speaking; 60+ events
  across 12 countries; audiences from 30-person workshops up to
  1,500-seat keynotes. Grounded, no-hype, always live demos.
- Talks: "AI Without the Hype" (keynote, 45 min);
  "Your First AI Workflow" (workshop, 3 hrs);
  "The Human Skills AI Can't Replace" (fireside, 30 min);
  "Adopting AI Responsibly" (panel, 60 min).
- Format needs: confidence monitor + stable wifi for live demos;
  can also present remotely. Travels from London. English.
- Fees: not published - depend on format and travel.

    Make it yours: replace every line under “Facts about me” with your own name, background, talks/projects, needs and anything you're happy to answer about. The more concrete the facts, the better the answers.

  3. Watch Claude render it as a live Artifact, and test it

    Claude builds the chat and it appears in the Artifact panel on the right — already interactive. Ask it three real questions, e.g. the ones below, and check every answer is true to your facts and never invented.

    What do you speak about?
Are you a fit for a fintech conference?
What do you need on stage?

    If an answer drifts or invents detail, tell Claude in the same chat — e.g. “don't mention any fee number” or “keep answers to 3 sentences” — and it rebuilds the artifact.

  4. Publish and share it

    Use the artifact's Publish / Share control to get a public link, then drop that link on your CV, site or email. Two things to know: viewers need their own Claude account (answers run on their account, so it isn't an anonymous public bot), and the live answers only work inside claude.ai — the same file opened as a plain web page can't reach Claude.

Subscription tip: everything here runs on a normal Claude subscription — no API key, no billing setup. “Create AI-powered artifacts” calls Claude for you at runtime, and each viewer's questions run on their own Claude plan.
